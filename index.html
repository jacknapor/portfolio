About Me

My name is Jack Napor, and I'm currently a computer science student at Bucknell University. I am finishing my senior year, and expect to graduate this May with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science.

My passion has always been for video games- both playing them and developing them. They have been present in my life since I was a young child, and their influence on me can not be understated. The unique ability video games hold as an art form to fully captivate and immerse its players in a interactive, living, breathing world was quickly evident to me. My experiences exploring these worlds served as my inspiration to focus my education around studying computer science. My academic pursuit of computer science began in high school, and continued through my education at Bucknell, all due to the beauty, wonder, camaraderie, and personal growth I experienced while immersed in these virtual worlds. My dream is to create my own worlds and tell stories through video games, in hopes that one day others can experience those same kinds of powerful, meaningful experiences that invoked the very passion I have built my life around.

What stemmed from this dream was a love for software development as a whole. From creating my own website and company, to developing an android application to help avid travelers, I have enjoyed exploring different paths within software engineering and am eager to continue learning, exploring and gathering experience with software development throughout my career.

Zoms

Introduction

As part of our Software Engineering and Design course, three other students and I created "Zoms", a 2D zombie survival game made in Java and inspired by the "Zombies" game mode in the Call of Duty series. After completing the course, I spent a significant amount of time polishing the UI, fixing bugs, and adding gameplay features. Features I added on my own include sound effects, music, and the damage animation.

Key Features

  • Survive waves of increasing amounts of zombies that gain speed every round.
  • Collect temporary upgrades that spawn around the map.
  • Earn points killing zombies and exchange them at the shop for ammo, health, armor or permanent upgrades.
  • Includes 3 different maps.
  • Try to reach the highest round you can before being overwhelmed.

TraveList

Introduction

TraveList is an android application developed to make it easier for users around the world to organize their travel plans and keep track of places they've visited. The idea was inspired by a Bucknell University professor who wanted an easy, user friendly way to keep track of which national parks he had visited and which he hadn't, and wanted it to be accessible on his mobile device to ensure easy access during his travels.

Key Features

  • Easily navigatable world map.
  • Search feature integrated with Google Maps API for easy location finding.
  • User list creation to organize travel locations.
  • Mark locations as visited and track progress towards visiting all locations in a list.
  • Displays any location in a list on the map.
  • Provides directions to any location in a list seamlessly with Google Maps.
  • Facebook Integration (Login/Sharing).

TripleHax

Introduction

During high school, I wanted a way to start combining my passion for gaming with my growing computer science knowledge. I decided to found a company, TripleHax LLC, and develop a website originally located at www.triplehax.com. I updated the site with reviews for video games I had played and with relevant news in the gaming industry. The website and company were recently dissolved, however the community generated about 500 likes on facebook and had 200 site members while it was active.

Key Features

  • User account creation & login.
  • Article search feature.
  • User comments on articles.
  • Facebook like/share capabilites for articles & website.
  • User access to an embedded arcade.
  • Integration with Google AdSense to support placement of ads throughout the site.
